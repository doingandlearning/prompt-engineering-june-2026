# AI-Assisted Development
## Prompt Engineering for Development Teams

**One day. Four modules. A shared foundation.**

<!-- end_slide -->

## Welcome

This day is for teams who are already aware of AI tools and want a common, well-grounded foundation — not an introduction from scratch.

By the end of today you will have:

- A working mental model for how AI tools work and where they fail
- Practical prompt engineering skills you can apply tomorrow
- Hands-on experience with code generation, explanation, refactoring, and testing
- A starting-point framework for consistent team-level AI use

<!-- end_slide -->

## Who this day is for

**Developers** working across Python (backend), React, Node, and PHP (frontend)

**Business analysts** who want to understand how requirements flow into AI-assisted development

No prior prompt engineering knowledge assumed. VS Code with AI integrations installed is expected.

<!-- end_slide -->

## The four modules

| # | Module | Focus | Duration |
|---|--------|-------|----------|
| 1 | GenAI and the Developer Landscape | Where to use AI safely | ~80 min |

<!-- end_slide -->

## How the day works

- **Lecture + live demo**: Facilitator explains and demonstrates in real time
- **Exercises**: You try it — individually or in pairs
- **Discussion**: We debrief what worked and what didn't

Every module opens with a problem. We solve it together before naming the framework.

<!-- end_slide -->

## What this day is not

- A comprehensive tour of every AI tool on the market
- A replacement for reading documentation or thinking carefully
- A guarantee that AI will produce correct code

AI changes what good work looks like. It does not change whether good work is needed.
